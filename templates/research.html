Quality of Experience Evaluation of SDN-Based Anomaly Detection on Real-Time Gaming Traffic

Lead Researcher

Dr. Aris V. Klonis

Published

Nov 2023

Network Protocol

SDN / OpenFlow

Technical Abstract

Exploring the intersection of packet-level anomalies and perceived user satisfaction in competitive cloud gaming environments.

Abstract

Software-Defined Networking (SDN) provides a dynamic platform for managing real-time traffic. However, detecting anomalies that specifically impact Quality of Experience (QoE) remains a challenge. This paper presents a novel framework that correlates network performance metrics directly with the ITU-T E-model for gaming.

We analyze how jitter, packet loss, and latency fluctuations during DDoS and flood attacks degrade the R-score of modern competitive titles. Our proposed detection model operates at the controller level, utilizing sFlow telemetry to preemptively reroute traffic before the user perceives a drop in responsiveness.
